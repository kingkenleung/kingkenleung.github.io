# Past Perfect Tense

# Use past perfect tense to show sequence of past actions

We usually use the past perfect tense when we want to make the order of past actions clear.

Compare:

  1. When Oliver arrived at the restaurant, Lily had already left.
  2. When Oliver arrived at the restaurant, Lily left.

The past perfect tense had already left has been used in Sentence 1 above to indicate that Oliver's arrival happened AFTER Lily's exit.

As for Sentence 2, simple past tense left has been used which implies that Oliver's arrival and Lily's exit happend almost at the same time. Lily is probably afraid of Oliver, isn't she? üòÜ

To conclude, the use of past perfect tense is to show that an action took place before another past action.

# Form of past perfect tense

The past perfect tense consists of an auxiliary verb had and a past participle. Check out the examples below,

I had paid.
We had won.
He had bought.

Check out more examples below,

I had been punished.
Ken had completed his homework.
Hongkongers had been highly cautious about COVID-19.

Pay special attention to the form of past perfect tense when passive voice is used.
